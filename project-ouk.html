Project Overview

Open University Kenya needed to transform their Moodle platform from a technical maze into an intuitive digital campus. The challenge: create a learning environment where students don’t get lost after the login screen. This involved reimagining navigation, course structure, and overall UX to make online education feel as natural as a physical classroom.

Moodle DevelopmentPHPUI/UX DesignCustom Development

Quick Facts

  • Role: Moodle Developer & UX Designer
  • Timeline: 8 weeks
  • Tools: Moodle, PHP, MySQL, Figma
  • Live Site: somasdev.ouk.ac.ke

The Challenge

The existing platform was a complex labyrinth that overwhelmed students and instructors alike. Navigation was confusing, layouts were inconsistent, and the experience felt more like solving a puzzle than learning.

Navigation Complexity

Students struggled to find courses and resources due to a confusing menu structure.

Inconsistent Design

Different courses had varying layouts, creating cognitive overhead for every user.

Poor Mobile Experience

The platform was difficult to use on mobile devices, severely limiting accessibility.

Design Process

01

Discovery & Analysis

Stakeholder interviews with students, faculty, and administrators. Analysed usage patterns and pain points in the existing system.

02

Information Architecture

Redesigned the entire site structure with clear hierarchy, intuitive navigation, and consistent course layouts.

03

UI/UX Design

Created a modern, clean interface focused on reducing cognitive load and improving the learning flow.

04

Development & Implementation

Built custom Moodle themes and plugins, ensuring responsive design and accessibility compliance.

Key Features

Intuitive Navigation

Streamlined menu with clear visual hierarchy and breadcrumb navigation for easy orientation.

Responsive Design

Fully mobile-optimised interface that works seamlessly across all devices and screen sizes.

Consistent Course Layout

Standardised course structure that reduces learning curve and improves content discoverability.

Accessibility First

WCAG compliant design with keyboard navigation, screen reader support, and high contrast options.

The transformed platform created a significant positive impact on student engagement and satisfaction.
